DESIGNER.

I’ve always had an affinity for visual art and a love of computers. When I began using software like Photoshop in highschool, I was sold on my future involving graphic design.

About Me

Hello, I’m Jeremy.

I’m a website developer and graphic designer based in London, Ontario. I’ve been involved in web development for about three years and have worked on a range of websites for small and mid-sized companies. I'm flexible with the projects I take on, but I most enjoy flexing my creative muscles and making unique, stand-out sites. DEVELOPER.

Computer coding makes me feel like the hacker in a heist movie. There is something incredibly satisfying about conceptualizing a feature and watching it come to fruition on the webpage. I’m also a passionate troubleshooter.

I’m well versed in using HTML and CSS to bring design mockups to life on the webpage. One of the things I enjoy the most about working with these languages is that they are always evolving, allowing me to use new tools and features as I improve as a web developer.

C'Angelina Catering

I worked with a local catering business to design and develop their website from start to finish. C'angelina's has no set menu and encourages customers to make custom requests that they fulfill using their commercial kitchen. With only minimal online presence, C'Angelinas tasked me with creating a website that consolidated and showcased their unique catering style. This was a great experience working with a smaller company that needed a lot of help modernizing their business.

Since this was their first website, I took the lead on creating the site structure and content. I met regularly to gather assets and details about their branding and their intended customer experience. I designed the webpage with their unique menu foremost in my mind, creating a Q&A page as well as a comprehensive home page to simplify user navigation. I designed an engaging page for each facet of their business they wished to highlight. I created a database to add their products dynamically to their menu page. I currently provide hosting services and server management for C'angelina.

Organ Donation

This organ donation proposal site was a group project during my time at Fanshawe College. The project required not only working as a group of designers and developers, but also working with and presenting our proposals to a marketing company partnered with the school and the Ontario government.

Each group member contributed to the design and building process. We were also tasked with creating videos and marketing assets that complimented the website. The main goal was to modernize the marketing approach for getting the opt-in organ donar sheets filled out. My primary role on this cooperative project was back-end development. I created the admin account management as well as the contact management system that allowed someone with no website development experience to edit text and media through their account. I also developed a section that allowed visitors to add a positive story of their own that could be cleared by an admin to show on the site on a carousel.

View Organ Donation

Data showed a lack of motivation to be the cause of low opt-in rates over an unwillingness to donate. Our marketing focused on emphasizing the speed at which someone could sign up and the impact it could have.

Most of my major contribution was the back-end work, managing databases and server requests. Every page has its content populated from my databases.

To allow the site owners to modify and maintain the content, I built an admin panel where master admins could manage other admins and pages could be manipulated.

Anomoly

Anomoly is a passion project that is designed to be a visual novel reader and game engine. I plan to use this experience to further my understanding of databases and RESTful APIs as a method of storing and retreiving content. Creating a game in this way helps broaden the scope of what I can code for clients. In addition to building something cool, my goal for this project is to further cement my grasp on server side code and operations.

The visual novel reader pulls all its data from an SQL database and populates each page as they are called for.

I created a story with art to facilitate the engine. My most recent addition is a "History" button that pulls the most recent dialogue and displays it in order.
